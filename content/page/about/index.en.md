## My journey

If there's one constant in what I do, it's change.
I started programming at 15 in 2016, like many, with the HTML/CSS course on OpenClassrooms, then I moved on to JS, then PHP, and then I learned to use MySQL. Everything was an excuse to create a website, and I didn't hold back. Good thing, because that's how I learn best.

Still, these technologies were enough for my first client projects starting in 2018, after high school classes. Well, that plus coffee and a colossal sleep debt. These were my first serious projects, and what fun they were! Seeing through a project that truly matters to someone, creating back-office solutions, micro-CMS, custom and fun interfaces; it was full-stack product development before the term even became popular.

After that, I went through university, but it wasn't really for me. I gained knowledge that I still use daily: systems, networks, database design, low-level programming.

## What drives me

As long as I find something interesting, I can dive down the rabbit hole and come out with knowledge. It's because I saw programming as modern magic that I started coding.

Ultimately, it's this desire to understand what I use and what surrounds me that makes me love sharing so much. Knowledge is like wine: if you keep it to yourself, you deprive others of it, and it eventually goes bad anyway. I don't believe in a world based on the artificial scarcity of knowledge.

Free software is a cause close to my heart, and I encourage everyone who can to reclaim their sovereignty over their tools by eliminating as many non-free components from their lives as possible. This sovereignty goes beyond software; the maker movement also resonates with me because it shares these same values of sovereignty and sharing.

Often, I participate in community events, like [Capitole du Libre](https://capitoledulibre.org/), [DevFest Toulouse](https://devfesttoulouse.fr/), [THCon](https://thcon.party), as well as various meetups announced on [Toulouse Tech Hub](https://toulouse-tech-hub.fr/).
Since 2025, I've been organizing the [Postgres Toulouse User Group](https://www.meetup.com/postgres-toulouse/) meetups with a team of volunteers.
